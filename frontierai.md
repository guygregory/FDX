# Frontier AI

## What is it?

The term **"Frontier AI"** was coined by OpenAI in 2023 to describe "highly capable foundation models that could possess dangerous capabilities sufficient to pose severe risks to public safety." Since its introduction, the "Frontier" terminology has been widely adopted as a recognised industry term for describing the most advanced AI models, especially in the context of risk, safety, and regulation.

## Why It Matters

Frontier AI models represent the cutting edge of artificial intelligence capability. These models:

- Push the boundaries of what AI systems can achieve in terms of performance and versatility.
- Require careful evaluation for potential risks to public safety and security.
- Demand robust governance frameworks to ensure responsible development and deployment.
- Shape policy discussions around AI regulation at national and international levels.

## Key Characteristics

- **Advanced capabilities:** Frontier models demonstrate state-of-the-art performance across a wide range of tasks.
- **Safety considerations:** These models undergo rigorous testing to identify and mitigate potential dangerous capabilities.
- **Regulatory focus:** Governments and industry bodies increasingly reference Frontier AI in developing safety standards and compliance requirements.
